to get started...

Prepared pianos is a technique that involves the non-destructive motification of a piano to alter its timbre. For the most part, prepared pianos (and other extended piano techniques) have been used in settings designed to showcase their unique sounds.

For my undergraduate thesis, I explored using extended piano techniques to fully replace instruments found in typical popular music arrangements in an attempt to play with the recontextualizion of a performance method generally relegated to the avant-garde.

so in the end...

A variety of extended piano techniques, including preparing the piano with screws, were employed. Fishing line was used to replace strings, and makeshift mallets were used to treat the strings like bells. Guest singers were also recruited to flesh out the project.

Eleven songs were written or adapted during the demo process; six of these songs were used for the project in the end, all of which were designed with different techniques in mind. Thematically, the songs all had to do with my own experiences with my undergraduate degree program.

 

The project was presented during the 2011 UCSD ICAM.best_of presentations.
